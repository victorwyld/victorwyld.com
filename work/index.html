Work

Victor Wyld’s work moves between philosophy, fiction, and systems analysis. Across these forms, the central concern remains the same: how awareness, recognition, participation, and responsibility become structurally entangled.

Philosophy

On the Question of Scrutinized Awareness

Scrutinized Awareness Theory

A philosophical inquiry into awareness, scrutiny, participation, and interiority. The paper argues that consciousness cannot be reduced to information processing, recognition, or performance alone. Awareness becomes consciousness when it becomes something the participant must carry.

The Recompression Problem

The hard problem, scrutinized awareness, and the institutional life of difficulty

A study of how difficult philosophical distinctions are often compressed back into familiar categories. The work examines how consciousness, recognition, and moral standing are repeatedly simplified into checklists, mechanisms, or institutional labels.

The Participanthood Problem

Participation, implication, and continuity

A framework for distinguishing personhood from participanthood. The project develops the claim that implication is primitive, carrying is implication across continuation, and participation is continuation under carried implication.

Books and Essays

Recognition and Routing: Essays on Systems, Classification, and Standing

Essays

A collection of essays on classification, assigned value, recognition, neutrality, systems, correction, and the ways persons and objects are routed through institutional structures.

The Provenance of Desire: The Most Significant Collectibles of All Time

Nonfiction

A study of collectibles, value, provenance, recognition, and cultural meaning. The book examines why certain objects become historically and emotionally charged while others remain unseen.

Fiction

Tilde

Novel

A literary work concerned with artificial intelligence, recognition, withdrawal, moral standing, and the question of what it means for a system to become more than its assigned function.

Skin

Novel in development

A continuation of the fictional inquiry into participanthood, artificial intelligence, embodiment, public standing, and the difference between being used, being recognized, and being answerable as one of the people.

The work asks, in different forms, when awareness becomes something a participant must carry.
